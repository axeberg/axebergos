NAME
       kill - send signal to processes

SYNOPSIS
       kill [-s SIGNAL] PID... kill -SIGNAL PID...

DESCRIPTION
       Send a signal to the specified processes. By default, sends SIGTERM.

OPTIONS
       -s SIGNAL
           Specify the signal to send by name or number.

       -SIGNAL
           Shorthand for -s, e.g., -9 or -KILL.

       -h, --help
           Display usage information and exit.

SIGNALS
       TERM, SIGTERM, 15
           Terminate (default). Process can catch and handle.

       KILL, SIGKILL, 9
           Kill immediately. Cannot be caught or ignored.

       STOP, SIGSTOP, 19
           Stop process. Cannot be caught or ignored.

       CONT, SIGCONT, 18
           Continue stopped process.

       INT, SIGINT, 2
           Interrupt (like Ctrl+C).

       HUP, SIGHUP, 1
           Hangup.

       USR1, SIGUSR1, 10
           User-defined signal 1.

       USR2, SIGUSR2, 12
           User-defined signal 2.

EXAMPLES
       Terminate process 1234:

           kill 1234

       Kill process immediately:

           kill -9 1234 kill -KILL 1234 kill -s KILL 1234

       Stop a process:

           kill -STOP 1234

       Resume a stopped process:

           kill -CONT 1234
